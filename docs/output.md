## About Isidore {#about_isidore}

Isidore is UD\'s open-source Learning Management System or LMS that runs
on the Sakai platform. Isidore is named after St. Isidore of Seville,
the patron saint of students and recommended for patronage of computers
and the Internet. The University of Dayton has been using Isidore since
the Fall semester in 2008.

If you are new to Isidore, we suggest clicking the button below to get a
better understanding of this system. \<br /\>\<br /\>
![fameless\|200px\|left\|Button that reads \"New to
Isidore\"\|link=New_To_Isidore](NewtoIsi.png "fameless|200px|left|Button that reads "New to Isidore"|link=New_To_Isidore")
\<br /\>\<br /\>\<br /\>

## Create a Blank Site from Scratch {#create_a_blank_site_from_scratch}

*This option allows you to create an empty (or blank) course site. If
you choose this option, you\'ll be able to select which tools are
installed in the course site.*

Please see this 2-minute video walkthrough of this process:\<br/\>
\<youtube\>oJj69vj3tF8\</youtube\>

1.  Click on the **Sites** menu (3x3 waffle icon) in the upper
    right-hand portion of the screen.
2.  Click on the **Create New Site** button.
3.  Under the \"Create a site from scratch\" option, select the academic
    term in which you\'ll be teaching and select **Continue**.
4.  Any courses that you\'re listed as the instructor of record will be
    displayed on the screen. To create a course site, select the check
    box next to the course you would like to create. *Please note that
    if you select multiple courses during this step, they will be
    combined into the same course site. If you would like to create
    separate course sites, you need to create your course sites one at a
    time.* Click on **Continue**.
    -   If you do not see your course(s) listed, click on the **I\'m
        unable to find my course or section\...** link and click on
        **Continue**. Select the \"Subject\", \"Course\", and
        \"Section\" from the drop-down menus and click on **Continue**.
    -   If you still don\'t see your course listed, click on the **Still
        cannot find your course/section?** link and manually enter the
        \"Subject\", \"Course\", and \"Section\" and click on
        **Continue**. A request will go to the Center for Online
        Learning who will either approve the request or will contact you
        for more information.
5.  Verify your name and email address on the next screen and click
    **Continue**.
6.  Check the box next to the tools you wish to use in your course site.
    *Please note that students are often annoyed if a tool sits empty
    all semester, so make sure that you\'re only selecting tools that
    will be used during the course.* Click **Continue**.
7.  Select how the course should be published (made available to
    students):
    -   Automatic - This is closest to what you\'ve experienced in the
        past. Isidore will automatically publish your site at 8:00 AM on
        the first day of the term and unpublish your site 2 weeks after
        the term ends.
    -   Scheduled - You can set a publish date/time for your site and an
        optional unpublish date/time. If you don\'t set an unpublish
        date/time, your site will remain available to students.
    -   Manual - You can manually publish and unpublish the site as you
        please.
8.  Click on **Continue**.
9.  Click on the **Create Site** button.
10. Your course site is now available. You may begin modifying it for
    the upcoming semester.

## Enabling Turnitin in Isidore\'s Assignments tool {#enabling_turnitin_in_isidores_assignments_tool}

1.  Navigate to the **Assignments** tool in your Isidore course site.
2.  Click on **Add**.
3.  Fill in the assignment title and instructions.
4.  If you would like students to acknowledge the honor pledge before
    submitting their work, check the box next to \"Add honor pledge\".
5.  If you would like to add attachments to the assignment that contains
    instructions or a template to complete the assignment, click on the
    **Add Attachments** button, browse to where the files are located,
    and upload the files.
6.  Set the open date, due date, and accept until date by clicking on
    the calendar and selecting a date. Once a date is selected, adjust
    the time by either using the slider or the minus and plus icons. The
    open date is when students will be able to see the assignment and
    begin submitting it. The accept until date is a date that students
    won\'t see, but allows them to submit their assignments late.
    Assignments submitted after the due date will be flagged as late.
7.  Set advanced date options, such as hiding the due date from students
    (not recommended), adding the due date to the calendar (only shown
    if the Calendar tool is installed), and/or adding an announcement
    about the open date to announcements (only shown if the
    Announcements tool is installed). If you enable the announcements
    option, you\'ll be able to choose whether to send an email
    notification about the open date to all participants.
8.  In the \"Assign To\" section, the settings allow you to select
    different options, such as individual assignments or group
    assignments.
    -   Assigning an assignment to each individual member of the site is
        the most frequently used option. This means that each student
        will submit their own assignment.
    -   Assigning an assignment to each individual member of the
        selected group(s) is used if you only want to assign an
        assignment to a specific group or section of students. Each
        student in the group or section will submit their own
        assignment.
    -   Assigning an assignment to the selected group(s), as a group
        assignment (one group member submits on behalf of the entire
        group) means that students are working in teams and only one
        student within that team will submit an assignment on behalf of
        the group.
9.  Submission type refers to the format of the student\'s submitted
    assignment.
    -   Attachments only is the most common type, where students can
        upload file attachment(s) for the instructor to review. Students
        may upload up to 5 file attachments per assignment.
    -   Inline means that students type or paste their assignment into a
        text box for the instructor to review.
    -   Inline and Attachments means that students can choose whether to
        upload a file attachment or type or paste their assignment into
        a text box.
    -   Non-electronic is used to provide assignment instructions, but
        students aren\'t expected to submit anything electronically. One
        use case might be bringing an article to class. Single file
        upload works just like the attachments type, but restricts the
        number of files students can upload to one.

    option. Once enabled, enter the number of resubmissions students are
    allowed to have and when those resubmissions should be due (usually
    the due date or before).

    default, students are allowed to see their originality reports
    (recommended), but you can uncheck that option.

## Reviewing Student TurnItIn Originality Reports {#reviewing_student_turnitin_originality_reports}

Once an assignment has been submitted it will be sent to TurnItIn to be
checked for plagiarism. An assignment can take anywhere from 15 minutes
to 24 hours to be reviewed in TurnItIn and returned to the Isidore
Assignments tool in your site.

1.  To review the reports you can click on the **Grade** link under the
    Assignment title.
2.  You'll see that a report is ready for your review when there's a
    flag icon under the **TurnItIn** column. Click on the flag icon next
    to the paper to open the Originality Report on TurnItIn's website.
    -   The color of the report icon indicates the similarity score of
        the paper, based on the amount of matching or similar text that
        was uncovered. The percentage range is 0% to 100%. The possible
        similarity ranges are:
        -   Blue: No matching text
        -   Green: One word to 24% matching text
        -   Yellow: 25-49% matching text
        -   Orange: 50-74% matching text
        -   Red: 75-100% matching text

-   **Please note that students\' submitted documents must be more than
    20 words for Turnitin to accept for review.**
-   **Please note that originality reports take at least 24 hours to be
    viewable for resubmission assignments.**
-   [View Instructor Guide for Interpreting Similarity Reports
    (PDF)](https://isidore.udayton.edu/access/content/public/eWiki%20Resources/Instructor%20Guide_Interpreting%20the%20Similarity%20Report.pdf)
-   [View Turnitin\'s online help for Interpreting Similarity
    Reports](https://help.turnitin.com/feedback-studio/turnitin-website/instructor/instructor-category.htm#TheSimilarityReport)

## How Turnitin Works {#how_turnitin_works}

It's important to review each Originality Report carefully -- just
because the similarity index number is high doesn't necessarily mean the
paper has been plagiarized. It's up to you as the instructor to review
the report and determine whether plagiarism has actually occurred.
TurnItIn will simply find matches of groups of text and show those to
you. It could very well be that student simply hasn't cited text
properly which makes it appear as copied.

TurnItIn looks for text matches of 7 words or more in three separate
areas:

-   Previously submitted papers to TurnItIn
-   Current and archived sites on the Internet
-   Collection of periodicals and journals -- some but not all

## Accessing Course Sites {#accessing_course_sites}

Course sites will appear under the **Sites** menu in Isidore. ![Sites
button](Sites.png "Sites button")

sites each semester. Not every faculty member chooses to use Isidore,

unpublished, but faculty are able to republish sites as needed.

Isidore registrations should be updated within 1-2 business days.

access.

    \"Add Question\".

1.  Click on the **Sites** icon (3x3 waffle) in the upper right-hand
    corner of your Isidore screen.
2.  Any site in which you are a participant should be displayed on the
    drop-down menu, which is separated by term.
3.  If you don\'t see a site, you may need to click on the **View All
    Sites** button near the top of the screen.

## Enable/Disable Autofavoriting of Course Sites {#enabledisable_autofavoriting_of_course_sites}

If you enable autofavoriting of course sites, sites you are added to or
sites you create will be automatically be shown at the top of your
Isidore screen.

To adjust autofavoriting preferences:

1.  Click on the **Sites** link next to your profile picture in the
    upper right-hand corner of the Isidore screen.
2.  Click on the **Organize Favorites** tab.
3.  Click on the **On/Off** toggle next to \"Automatically add new sites
    to your favorites bar.\"

## Manually Favoriting Course Sites {#manually_favoriting_course_sites}

1.  Click on the **Sites** link next to your profile picture in the
    upper right-hand corner of the Isidore screen.
2.  Click on the star next to the course sites you would like to
    favorite.
3.  Click on the blue **X** to close your Sites drawer.
4.  You may need to click on the **Reload** link for the favorited
    course(s) to appear in the blue Isidore bar at the top of the
    screen.

## Reordering Course Sites {#reordering_course_sites}

1.  Click on the **Sites** link next to your profile picture in the
    upper right-hand corner of the Isidore screen.
2.  Click on the **Organize Favorites (#)** tab.
3.  Drag the courses up and down until you get them into your preferred
    order.
4.  Click on the blue **X** to close your Sites drawer.
5.  You may need to click on the **Reload** link for the favorited
    course(s) to appear in the correct order in the blue Isidore bar at
    the top of the screen.

## Adjust Dates Using the \"Shift By\" Feature {#adjust_dates_using_the_shift_by_feature}

The \"shift dates by\" feature allows you to bump all dates within your
site by a set number of days.

**NOTE:** This feature can only update dates that are set in the system,
not dates manually typed in by the user such as within assignment
instructions, page titles, etc. \<br/\>\<br/\>See a step-by-step video
of this process: \<br/\>\<youtube\>BWW14c1yKqI\</youtube\>

1.  If you haven't already done so, we recommend first following the
    [steps to duplicate your site](Site_Creation "wikilink") to the new
    semester.\</br\>\</br\>
2.  Navigate to the **Site Info** tool in your site and click on the
    **Date Manager** tab.\</br\>\</br\>
3.  Evaluate the existing dates in your site by expanding each tool to
    determine which dates you want to shift forward. You probably do
    **NOT** want to shift the dates for the Calendar or Announcements
    tools, but it would make sense to shift dates for Assignments, Tests
    & Quizzes, Lessons, and Forums. \</br\>\</br\>
4.  Determine the number of days to shift by.
    -   Find the earliest date in your class from the previous semester
        and match it to the equivalent date in the next semester. For
        example, if the first Lesson for the class opened on August
        23rd, 2021, I would then want the first Lesson for the class in
        the next semester to open on January 10th, 2022.
    -   Use the [date duration
        calculator](https://www.timeanddate.com/date/duration.html) to
        determine the number of days in between those two dates. Hint:
        do NOT select the \"Include end date in calculation\" option.

:   

    :   ![Steps to Calculate Number of Days using Date
        Calculator](DateDuration.png "Steps to Calculate Number of Days using Date Calculator")

1.  \<li value=\"4\"\>Once you have the number of days to shift by, make
    sure to expand only the tools to which you'd like to apply the date
    shift.\</br\>\</br\>
2.  Type in the number of days to shift by and click the "Apply to
    expanded sections only" button. \</br\>\</br\>

:   

    :   ![Using the Shift By
        Feature](ShiftBy.png "Using the Shift By Feature")

1.  \<li value=\"5\"\>Spot check a few dates to see if the dates shifted
    as you would expect. If the dates mostly look correct, press **Save
    Changes** at the bottom. If they do not, just press **Cancel**.
    \</br\>\</br\>
    -   **NOTE:** If it turns out your dates are not correct and you
        have already pressed Save Changes, you can \"undo\" the action
        by shifting the dates by putting a minus sign in front of the
        number (e.g. -140) and applying it. \</br\>\</br\>
2.  Fine-tune any dates that need to be adjusted to account for a
    different academic calendar. Refer to our [Module Date
    Planner](https://isidore.udayton.edu/access/content/public/Module%20Date%20Planner-Summer2021-Spring2022.xlsx)
    to see all the dates for the semester.

## Adjust Dates Manually {#adjust_dates_manually}

1.  Navigate to the **Site Info** tool in your Isidore course site.
2.  Click on the tool link to expand the section and view items within
    that tool.
3.  Click on the **calendar** icon to edit dates with the date picker by
    selecting year, then month, then day. Use the time sliders or **+**
    and **-** buttons to adjust times.
4.  You can update the dates of multiple tools at the same time.
5.  When you\'re done making changes, click on the **Save Changes**
    button near the bottom of the page.
6.  If the dates have been successfully updated, a confirmation message
    will be displayed on the screen.
    -   If some dates couldn\'t be updated, an error message will be
        displayed on the screen. Check the date and time format to make
        sure it\'s correct and click on **Save Changes** again. You may
        need to update these dates manually.
